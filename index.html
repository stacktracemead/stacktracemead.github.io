About

Who I Am

I'm a geek who obsesses over the details in my craft, whether that craft is technology or home meadmaking. Having sampled countless craft beers, I see modern mead not only as a canvas on which to create interesting flavors but also as the next big genre in craft beverages. I love experimenting with new ingredients in my recipes, drawing inspiration from flavors found at home in Chicago and from my travels. Whether you're a geek like me or you're brand new to mead, I'd love to share a bottle. Cheers!

What's With the Name?

Stack Trace is a nod to my roots - it's the deep detail that software engineers use to troubleshoot issues. 🤓

Proxy Continuation

Metheglin | 12.7%
Valkyrie's Horn 2020 - Silver Medal
Orpheus MeadFest 2019 - Gold Medal

Inspired by CH Distillery's OxyContin cocktail (now renamed to Cease & Desist), this metheglin combines wildflower Gentle Breeze Honey from Mt. Horeb, WI with Lapsang Souchong tea from Rare Tea Cellar, juniper berries, ginger, and lemon zest.

  • Batch 2 - May 2020, 11%

Reid

Melomel | 15%
Reserve Series

Reid is a dessert mead where the only liquid comes from the over ten pounds of fruit per gallon of finished mead. It borrows from my favorite berry melomel recipes, Heartbleed and EternalBlue, and uses Michigan Bluecrop blueberries, Michigan red and black raspberries, and Florida orange blossom honey. A diversion from my normal naming convention, I crafted this mead as a tribute to the sweetest little addition to my life, who joined us on June 29th, 2020.
